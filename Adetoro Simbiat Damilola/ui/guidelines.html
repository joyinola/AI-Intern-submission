American Cancer Society (ACS) Breast Cancer Screening
2023 Update
Screening

The ACS 2023 guidelines form the backbone of Check Me's routine and elevated-tier recommendations:

Women aged 40–44 have the option to start annual breast cancer screening with mammograms.
Women 45–54 should get mammograms every year.
Women 55 and older can switch to mammograms every 2 years, or continue annual screening.
Women at high risk (BRCA mutation, prior radiation, strong family history) should get annual MRI alongside mammography.
Palpable lump + strong family history combination triggers urgent imaging referral per ACS guidance.

NICE NG101 — Familial Breast Cancer Management
Updated 2023
Familial Risk

NICE NG101 informs Check Me's BRCA mutation flags and high-risk surveillance recommendations:

Annual MRI surveillance from age 30 for women with BRCA1/2 mutations (triggered by age_brca flag).
Annual MRI from age 40 for women with a ≥30% lifetime risk based on family history.
Referral to specialist genetics service for women with ≥10% probability of BRCA mutation.
Chemoprevention options (tamoxifen, anastrozole) discussed for high-risk premenopausal women.

ACR BI-RADS — Breast Imaging Reporting and Data System
5th Edition
Imaging

BI-RADS 5th edition informs Check Me's FNA feature interpretation and imaging-based flags:

Dense breast tissue (BI-RADS categories C/D) is a recognised independent risk factor surfaced in the dense_breast field.
Skin thickening or nipple discharge combined with skin changes are associated with inflammatory breast cancer per BI-RADS descriptors (triggers skin_nipple IMMEDIATE flag).
FNA nucleus measurements (radius, area, concavity) are derived from BI-RADS cytological assessment frameworks.

UCI Wisconsin Breast Cancer Diagnostic Dataset
Wolberg, Street & Mangasarian — 1995
Dataset

The FNA nucleus measurements in Check Me are calibrated to the distributions in this landmark dataset. 26 features including radius, texture, perimeter, area, smoothness, compactness, concavity, concave points, symmetry, and fractal dimension are computed from digitised fine needle aspirate images.

Original dataset: 569 cases (212 malignant, 357 benign) from the University of Wisconsin Hospital.
Check Me's synthetic training dataset of 3,000 patients mirrors these feature distributions with ~35% malignant prevalence to simulate a screening-positive referral population.
