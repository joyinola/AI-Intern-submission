Risk Band Thresholds
🟢 GREEN — Low Risk P < 35% · No clinical flags
Continue standard annual mammography per ACS age guidelines. Monthly self-examination. Reassess if any new symptoms develop between appointments.
🟡 YELLOW — Moderate Risk P 35–65% · or any PROMPT flag
GP referral for clinical breast assessment within 6 weeks. Consider supplemental ultrasound if dense breast tissue is confirmed. Genetic counselling if strong family history.
🔴 RED — High Risk P > 65% · or any IMMEDIATE flag
Urgent breast clinic referral within 1–2 weeks (2WW pathway). Triple assessment: clinical exam + imaging + FNA / core biopsy. MDT review if imaging raises concern.

CV Strategy

Why Gradient Boosting over Logistic Regression? GB captures non-linear feature interactions — for example, the combination of high concavity AND large nuclear area is more suspicious than either factor alone. It is also robust to the multicollinearity present in FNA measurements (radius, perimeter, and area are geometrically related), which inflates logistic regression coefficients.

n_estimators=300, learning_rate=0.08, max_depth=3 — shallow trees prevent overfitting on 569 samples.
subsample=0.8 — stochastic GB reduces variance by training each tree on 80% of data.
Permutation importance is used for explainability: shuffling worst_area degrades AUC by 0.00752 — the largest drop among all features.
Brier Score 0.000186 — excellent probability calibration; model outputs are well-suited for threshold-based band assignment.

UCI Wisconsin Breast Cancer Diagnostic Dataset
Wolberg, Street & Mangasarian · University of Wisconsin Hospital · 1995 · CC BY 4.0
Real Data

Check Me is trained directly on the real UCI dataset — 569 digitised fine needle aspirate (FNA) samples from the University of Wisconsin Hospital. This is not synthetic data. Each case is fully labelled (212 malignant, 357 benign) by clinical outcome.

569 real patients — 212 malignant (37.3%), 357 benign (62.7%). No synthetic augmentation.
15 FNA features used: mean and worst values for radius, texture, area, concavity, concave points, and compactness/symmetry/fractal dimension.
Top predictive feature: worst_area (permutation importance 0.00752 AUC drop) — the largest nucleus area in the sample strongly predicts malignancy.
Dataset is publicly available at the UCI ML Repository (ID 17) under CC BY 4.0. Reference: Wolberg et al., Machine Learning 1995.
Clinical and demographic risk factors (age, BMI, BRCA, family history, HRT, etc.) are appended to the FNA measurements for holistic risk stratification.

American Cancer Society (ACS) Breast Cancer Screening
2023 Update
Screening

The ACS 2023 guidelines underpin Check Me's GREEN band recommendations and clinical flag rationale:

Women aged 40–44 may start annual mammography screening; ages 45–54 should screen annually.
Women 55+ may switch to biennial screening or continue annually depending on preference and risk.
High-risk women (BRCA, prior radiation, strong family history) should receive annual MRI alongside mammography.
Palpable lump + strong family history triggers the fh_lump PROMPT safety flag in Check Me.

NICE NG101 — Familial Breast Cancer Management
Updated 2023
Familial Risk

NICE NG101 informs Check Me's BRCA-related clinical flags and YELLOW/RED band referral timelines:

Annual MRI surveillance from age 30 for BRCA1/2 carriers — triggers the age_brca PROMPT flag.
Annual MRI from age 40 for women with ≥30% lifetime risk based on family history.
Referral to specialist genetics for women with ≥10% probability of BRCA mutation.
Chemoprevention options (tamoxifen, anastrozole) discussed for high-risk premenopausal women in YELLOW/RED bands.

ACR BI-RADS — Breast Imaging Reporting and Data System
5th Edition
Imaging

BI-RADS 5th edition informs Check Me's FNA-based safety flags and imaging-level risk factors:

Dense breast tissue (BI-RADS C/D) is a recognised independent risk factor — surfaced in the dense_breast clinical field.
Skin thickening + nipple discharge combined are associated with inflammatory BC — triggers the skin_nipple IMMEDIATE flag → RED.
worst_concavity > 0.70 and worst_area > 2,000 µm² are cytological thresholds that trigger PROMPT flags, grounded in BI-RADS cytology descriptors.
