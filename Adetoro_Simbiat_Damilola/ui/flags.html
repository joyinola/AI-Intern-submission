How Safety Flags Work

Safety flags are evaluated before the machine learning model. They are hard-coded clinical rules grounded in published oncology guidelines. When a flag is triggered, it is always surfaced to the clinician — regardless of the ML probability score.

IMMEDIATE flags (red) override the ML output and force the URGENT risk tier. These represent combinations of findings that require same-day clinical action.

PROMPT flags (amber) escalate the risk tier to at least HIGH. If the ML model already scores HIGH or URGENT, the flag adds additional clinical context without changing the tier.
